2024 ASEE Beaver Bot

The competition

The 2024 ASEE Beaver Bot Competition, held in Portland, Oregon, tasked teams with designing robots that autonomously follow a line around a track while performing a series of tasks. The robots were required to "cut down trees," store them, and then transport and dump them into a river or pond to accumulate points. Teams were judged based on their robot’s ability to complete these tasks efficiently, with the highest-scoring team declared the winner. The competition emphasized precision engineering, creativity, and teamwork, providing students with a practical, hands-on application of robotics and programming skills.

Design

We start by brainstorming and designing our robots using 3D CAD software like Fusion 360. This stage is all about creativity and problem-solving, as we work to develop models that meet the needs of our competition challenges.

Manufacturing

Once the design is finalized, we bring our ideas to life by manufacturing the parts. This often involves 3D printing, laser cutting, or other fabrication methods to create precise components. Members learn how to handle various tools and techniques, making this stage a great introduction to hands-on engineering.

Assembly & Electronics

Next, we wire up our robots, soldering components and connecting sensors, motors, and microcontrollers. This step is crucial as it brings together the hardware that powers our designs.

Programming

While the physical robot takes shape, we code its functionality using Arduino C. Programming is where the magic happens; we write the software that controls the robot’s movements, sensor responses, and overall behavior.

Testing & Iteration

Building robots is an iterative process. We continuously test our designs, refine the code, and make adjustments to improve performance. This back-and-forth approach is a great way to learn from mistakes, innovate, and perfect our robots before competition day.
